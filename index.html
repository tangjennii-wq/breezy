Careyes & Cuixmala

Costalegre, Pacific coast, Jalisco, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Dominican Republic

The vibe: Private architectural estates on a wild coastline — quiet, curated and singular. Horses, nature preserves, empty beaches and dramatic scenery; more a private estate than a hotel.

The wild-and-artful coast: 35,000 acres of protected reserve, nine miles of empty beaches, swimmable coves, and a bohemian community of clifftop "ocean castles" and casitas. It's the purest casita answer — and the most remote, so it rewards settling in and staying put.

Below: an area map with drive times, where to stay (Careyes villas, the Cuixmala estate, and Las Alamandas), the water and wildlife, a proposed week, and an honest budget — this is the priciest finalist.

The Area — Beaches, Wildlife & More

A remote, spread-out coast. Manzanillo (ZLO, ~1–1½h) is the nearest airport; Puerto Vallarta (PVR, ~2½–3h) is the other gateway, off-map to the north.

  • Beaches & coves (Playa Rosa, Careyitos) — at the door
  • Chamela-Cuixmala reserve safari — on the estate
  • Careyes Polo Club & beach club — ~10 min
  • Copa del Sol (boat or drive) — short hop
  • Tenacatita snorkel cove — ~30 min
  • El Tamarindo golf (Four Seasons) — ~20 min
  • Las Alamandas — ~40 min north
  • Manzanillo airport (ZLO) — ~1–1½h

Splurge & alternatives

A few more — mostly beyond budget, or better as a side option:

  • Casa Sol de Occidente — the other clifftop "ocean castle," priced in the same over-budget range.
  • Las Alamandas — an artful boutique estate ~40 min north with its own beaches; ~$377–$2,865/night per unit or a whole-estate buyout by quote.

On the Water & in the Wild

This is the coast's real draw. Swimmable coves for kayaking, paddleboarding and snorkeling, boat trips along the shore to the cliff-top Copa del Sol, and — because you're going in late December — the start of whale-watching season. Add sea-turtle releases and a horseback safari through the Chamela-Cuixmala reserve past zebra, eland and a crocodile lagoon. It's ocean rather than rivers, so no whitewater here — but plenty of water and wildlife.

Los Cabos

Baja California Sur, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Dominican Republic

The vibe: The resort end of the spectrum — beach clubs, golf, pools, spas and restaurants, where the hotel is the destination. The most conventional option (kept for easy group villas).

The easiest trip of the finalists — a ~6½h nonstop from Boston, no red-eye, no connection — with the best food-and-farm scene and, thanks to the Sea of Cortez, the strongest water of any Mexico option. The trade-off is cost: it's the priciest finalist at peak.

For eight you'd take a private villa in the gated communities (Pedregal, Palmilla, Cabo del Sol) — Acre's treehouses are couples-sized, so it's a hacienda or a villa. The quieter Todos Santos / El Pescadero pocket is now its own section.

The Area — Beaches, Reef, Farms & Golf

Most stays cluster along the corridor between San José del Cabo and Cabo San Lucas; Todos Santos is ~1h up the Pacific. Los Cabos airport (SJD) is central.

  • Acre farm & restaurant — ~15 min from San José
  • San José del Cabo (art town) — ~15–20 min
  • Chileno & Santa María snorkel bays — ~20–30 min
  • Puerto Los Cabos golf — ~15 min
  • Pedregal / Cabo San Lucas — ~30–40 min
  • Sierra de la Laguna / Fox Canyon hike — ~45 min
  • Cabo Pulmo reef (snorkel/dive) — ~1h

On the Water

The best water of the Mexico finalists. Cabo Pulmo (~1h) is a protected national park with the healthiest coral reef in the Sea of Cortez — world-class snorkeling and diving among huge schools of fish, rays and turtles. Closer in, the Chileno and Santa María bays are calm, swimmable snorkel coves. Late December is prime whale-watching season (humpbacks and grays), and there's surf at Cerritos near Todos Santos plus easy kayak/SUP in the bays. No rivers, so no whitewater — but the ocean more than makes up for it.

Todos Santos & El Pescadero

Baja California Sur, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Dominican Republic

The vibe: Baja surf town meets boutique design hotel — artsy and laid-back, desert landscapes, farm-to-table, and a slower pace than Cabo.

The quiet, design-forward side of Baja: an hour up the Pacific from the Los Cabos airport, Todos Santos and neighboring El Pescadero trade the Cabo villa-and-golf scene for organic farms, empty surf beaches and understated design hotels under the Sierra de la Laguna. Same easy ~6½h nonstop from Boston — a completely different feel.

For eight you'd take a small design-hotel (Paradero, Rancho Pescadero, Villa Santa Cruz) or a private villa in El Pescadero. It's the most on-brief Baja option: farm-driven, low-key, and genuinely not a resort bubble.

The Area — Beach, Farms & Town

A small farm-and-surf pocket on the Pacific, ~1h up from the Los Cabos airport (SJD). El Pescadero and Todos Santos are ~15 min apart.

  • El Pescadero farms & hotels — at the door

  • Todos Santos town (galleries, Hotel California) — ~15 min
  • Sierra de la Laguna hikes — ~30–45 min
  • Los Cabos airport (SJD) — ~1h
  • Cabo San Lucas / corridor — ~1h

San Miguel de Allende

Colonial highlands, Guanajuato, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Dominican Republic

The vibe: A colonial-city escape — architecture, food, art and courtyards, boutique hotels. More cultural than nature-focused.

Our first pick, and the easiest to make work: a beautiful, walkable town with wonderful food, a real wine route on the doorstep, comfortable dry-season weather, and simple logistics for a group — all without feeling like a resort.

On the vines (alternative)

If the estate-among-the-vines feeling wins over being in town: La Santísima Trinidad (a 4BR residence plus rooms, ~20 min out) or Viñedo San Miguel (~10 min out).

On the Water

San Miguel is high-desert, so the water here is gentle rather than whitewater. On Presa Allende, the reservoir ~15 min southwest of town, you can kayak, canoe, paddleboard and sail — Amigos de la Presa runs guided paddles, and it's an easy, pretty half-day. There's no river tubing or whitewater rafting nearby (the closest rafting is the Huasteca, a long haul away). If river adventures like the ones you loved in Costa Rica are a must, that's where Jarabacoa (whitewater rafting, canyoning, river days) and El Yunque (rainforest rivers & natural pools) pull ahead.

Dominican Republic — North Coast

Sea Horse Ranch + Jarabacoa adventures · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Dominican Republic

The vibe: A tropical adventure estate — villa living, horseback, beaches, waterfalls, rivers, rafting and surf. Active and outdoorsy, less design-focused.

Our DR pick: base on the quiet north coast at Sea Horse Ranch — a gated community of private beach villas with full service, ~20 min from Puerto Plata (direct flights from Boston) and far from the mainstream crowds. It stacks up the water and adventure you're after: the 27 Waterfalls of Damajagua (canyoning & jumping), Cabarete's kite/surf/SUP beaches, El Choco caves, snorkeling, and a whitewater-rafting day-trip up to Jarabacoa (~2h).

One honest note: this is a beach-and-adventure week, not a wine one — the north coast isn't a wine region (Ocoa Bay's vineyard is ~3.5h away). If wine matters more than the beach, the alternative is to base up in the Jarabacoa mountains (rustic) instead.

The Area — Beach, Falls & Rivers

The north coast is low-key and green, ~20 min from Puerto Plata's airport (POP, direct flights from Boston). The mountains and rafting are a ~2h day-trip south.

  • Sea Horse Ranch beach & Cabarete — at the door
  • Puerto Plata airport (POP) — ~20 min
  • 27 Waterfalls of Damajagua (canyoning) — ~45 min
  • Sosúa beaches & snorkeling — ~15 min
  • Playa Grande golf (Río San Juan) — ~1h
  • Jarabacoa — whitewater rafting & hikes — ~2h
  • Samaná (whales, from mid-Jan) — ~3h

On the Water & Adventure

This coast is loaded with the water-and-adventure you loved. The 27 Waterfalls of Damajagua (~45 min) is a canyoning romp of jumps and natural slides; Cabarete is the Caribbean's kite/surf/SUP capital; there's snorkeling and diving off Sosúa, El Choco caves to explore, and horseback along the sand. For the whitewater itch, a day-trip up to Jarabacoa (~2h) rafts the Río Yaque del Norte — the only real whitewater on the whole shortlist. Beaches are swimmable and calm at the coves. The one gap is wine (no vineyard on this coast); whale season in Samaná starts ~mid-January, just after your dates.
