Winter Break

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: not a generic resort vacation — we want places that feel curated, design-forward, immersed in nature and experiential, where the destination itself is the experience (Babylonstoren) rather than just a base for excursions. The options fall into two directions: the Caribbean & East Coast (shorter flights) and Pacific Mexico. This page is the general overview — open any section above to deep-dive each destination.

Caribbean & East Coast

Shorter flights from Boston — and where all our favorites are, roughly in order of preference.

  • Puerto Rico (Royal Isabela & Vieques) — a working farm-estate of cliff-top casitas with farm-to-table dining, plus the design guesthouse Finca Victoria on Vieques; ~4h nonstop, no passport.
  • Grenada (Six Senses La Sagesse & Calabash) — a design/wellness resort with an organic garden, plus locally-owned cocoa & spice estates, rainforest waterfalls and quiet beaches.
  • British Virgin Islands (Virgin Gorda) — Rosewood Little Dix Bay and Oil Nut Bay, with The Baths, sailing and Guana Island as a private nature-sanctuary alternative.
  • Dominican Republic (Playa Grande & Sea Horse Ranch) — a design-forward beach base (the storybook Playa Grande bungalows) or a roomier full-service villa (Sea Horse Ranch), with waterfalls, reefs and rafting; ~4h nonstop.

Dominica's Secret Bay (secretbay.dm) was a beautiful option — clifftop rainforest villas on the Nature Island — but a full week at holiday time books up far in advance, so it'd be one to plan a year ahead, sadly.

Pacific Mexico

  • Careyes / Cuixmala — private architectural estates on a wild coastline; horses, nature preserves, empty beaches and dramatic scenery. Curated and singular.
  • Todos Santos / El Pescadero — Baja surf town meets boutique design hotel; artsy, desert, farm-to-table, slow.
  • San Miguel de Allende — the highlands option: a colonial city of architecture, food, art and courtyards, on a real wine route. More cultural than coastal.
  • Los Cabos — the resort end: beach clubs, golf, pools and spas, where the hotel is the destination.

Also on the radar but doesn't seem to be the vibe: Riviera Maya / Tulum, the US Virgin Islands (Lovango), and Turks & Caicos.

Best Hikes

The standout trail near each option, ordered by an overall hiking score (★★★★★ = best) — my own consistent read across all eight, weighing trail variety, scenery, accessibility from the house and how family-friendly it is. Open the AllTrails link for the official map, distance and difficulty; the review count in brackets is just a popularity signal (how well-trodden the trail is), which is why it varies so much and isn't a quality score.

  • Puerto Rico — El Yunque Trail  ★★★★★ — a full-day climb through the only tropical rainforest in the U.S. forest system to the misty peak, inside a large, well-maintained trail network with easier options for everyone; by far the most-hiked trail of any option here. AllTrails · El Yunque Trail [2,228] · all El Yunque trails
  • Dominican Republic — Pico Duarte  ★★★★★ — the Caribbean's highest peak (3,098 m), a serious multi-day guided trek from La Ciénaga near Jarabacoa; the best raw hiking of any option, though the summit is a real expedition rather than a casual walk. Gentler alternative for the group: the 27 Charcos de Damajagua river-scramble. AllTrails · Pico Duarte [86]
  • Grenada — Seven Sisters Waterfalls  ★★★★★ — a rainforest walk in Grand Étang National Park to a chain of falls and pools you can jump into, part of a decent network of rainforest and crater-lake trails; short but atmospheric. AllTrails · Seven Sisters [190]
  • Los Cabos / East Cape — Fox Canyon (Cañón de la Zorra)  ★★★★★ — a shaded canyon walk in the Sierra de la Laguna foothills to the Sol de Mayo waterfall and swimming pools; easy, rewarding and very family-friendly. AllTrails · La Zorra Canyon [123]
  • Todos Santos / El Pescadero — Sierra de la Laguna  ★★★★★ — palm-canyon backcountry rising into pine-oak highlands above the desert; the San Dionisio canyon route is the classic, though it's more remote and less maintained than the Cabo side. AllTrails · Sierra de la Laguna [27]
  • San Miguel de Allende — Peña de Bernal  ★★★★★ — a short steep scramble up one of the world's tallest monoliths, an easy day trip on the wine route, plus the in-town Charco del Ingenio canyon-and-botanical-garden loop; pleasant day walks rather than immersive wilderness. AllTrails · Peña de Bernal [256] · Charco del Ingenio [67]
  • British Virgin Islands — Gorda Peak  ★★★★★ — a shaded climb through national-park forest to a lookout tower with 360° views over the islands and sailing waters; a quick half-day outing, and about the only real trail on the island. AllTrails · Gorda Peak Trail [58]
  • Careyes / Cuixmala  ★★★★★ — mostly a private coastal nature reserve, so mapped trails are limited; the Cerro Careyes viewpoint is the main marked walk, and most nature outings here are guided within the estate. AllTrails · Cerro Careyes [6]

Careyes & Cuixmala

Costalegre, Pacific coast, Jalisco, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: Private architectural estates on a wild coastline — quiet, curated and singular. Horses, nature preserves, empty beaches and dramatic scenery; more a private estate than a hotel.

The wild-and-artful coast: 35,000 acres of protected reserve, nine miles of empty beaches, swimmable coves, and a bohemian community of clifftop "ocean castles" and casitas. It's the purest casita answer — and the most remote, so it rewards settling in and staying put.

Below: an area map with drive times, where to stay (Careyes villas, the Cuixmala estate, and Las Alamandas), the water and wildlife, a proposed week, and an honest budget — this is the priciest finalist.

The Area — Beaches, Wildlife & More

A remote, spread-out coast. Manzanillo (ZLO, ~1–1½h) is the nearest airport; Puerto Vallarta (PVR, ~2½–3h) is the other gateway, off-map to the north.

  • Beaches & coves (Playa Rosa, Careyitos) — at the door
  • Chamela-Cuixmala reserve safari — on the estate
  • Careyes Polo Club & beach club — ~10 min
  • Copa del Sol (boat or drive) — short hop
  • Tenacatita snorkel cove — ~30 min
  • El Tamarindo golf (Four Seasons) — ~20 min
  • Las Alamandas — ~40 min north
  • Manzanillo airport (ZLO) — ~1–1½h

Los Cabos

Baja California Sur, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: The resort end of the spectrum — beach clubs, golf, pools, spas and restaurants, where the hotel is the destination. The most conventional option (kept for easy group villas).

The easiest trip of the finalists — a ~6½h nonstop from Boston, no red-eye, no connection — with the best food-and-farm scene and, thanks to the Sea of Cortez, the strongest water of any Mexico option. The trade-off is cost: it's the priciest finalist at peak.

For eight you'd take a private villa in the gated communities (Pedregal, Palmilla, Cabo del Sol) — Acre's treehouses are couples-sized, so it's a hacienda or a villa. The quieter Todos Santos / El Pescadero pocket is now its own section.

The Area — Beaches, Reef, Farms & Golf

Most stays cluster along the corridor between San José del Cabo and Cabo San Lucas; Todos Santos is ~1h up the Pacific. Los Cabos airport (SJD) is central.

  • Acre farm & restaurant — ~15 min from San José
  • San José del Cabo (art town) — ~15–20 min
  • Chileno & Santa María snorkel bays — ~20–30 min
  • Puerto Los Cabos golf — ~15 min
  • Pedregal / Cabo San Lucas — ~30–40 min
  • Sierra de la Laguna / Fox Canyon hike — ~45 min

Todos Santos & El Pescadero

Baja California Sur, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: Baja surf town meets boutique design hotel — artsy and laid-back, desert landscapes, farm-to-table, and a slower pace than Cabo.

The quiet, design-forward side of Baja: an hour up the Pacific from the Los Cabos airport, Todos Santos and neighboring El Pescadero trade the Cabo villa-and-golf scene for organic farms, empty surf beaches and understated design hotels under the Sierra de la Laguna. Same easy ~6½h nonstop from Boston — a completely different feel.

For eight you'd take a small design-hotel (Paradero, Rancho Pescadero, Villa Santa Cruz) or a private villa in El Pescadero. It's the most on-brief Baja option: farm-driven, low-key, and genuinely not a resort bubble.

The Area — Beach, Farms & Town

A small farm-and-surf pocket on the Pacific, ~1h up from the Los Cabos airport (SJD). El Pescadero and Todos Santos are ~15 min apart.

  • El Pescadero farms & hotels — at the door

  • Sierra de la Laguna hikes — ~30–45 min
  • Los Cabos airport (SJD) — ~1h
  • Cabo San Lucas / corridor — ~1h

Dominican Republic — North Coast

Playa Grande & Sea Horse Ranch, north coast · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: a design-forward beach base on the quiet north coast, with a whole lot of water and adventure at the door — waterfalls, reefs, kite/surf, horseback and a whitewater day-trip. Two very different homes for the week, and the choice comes down to vibe vs. easy group living.

Lead pick — Playa Grande Beach Club: the design choice, and the most on-brief. Nine storybook bungalows by the Kemble family on a wild mile-long Atlantic beach at Río San Juan, with its own restaurant, pool and the Robert Trent Jones golf course next door. It photographs like a magazine, and fits eight (book the three-bedroom bungalows or a full buyout).

Or Sea Horse Ranch: the roomier, full-service alternative — one big oceanfront villa with a private pool, staff and in-villa chef, in a gated community with a beach club, calmer swimming coves and quicker access to the Cabarete/Damajagua adventure hub. Less designed, but easier for a group and better for swimming.

Best of both — a split stay: 3 nights at Playa Grande on the coast, then 2 nights at a boutique mountain lodge in Jarabacoa for whitewater rafting, waterfalls and cool mountain air.

The Area — Beach, Falls & Rivers

The north coast is low-key and green, ~20 min from Puerto Plata's airport (POP, direct flights from Boston). The mountains and rafting are a ~2h day-trip south.

  • Sea Horse Ranch beach & Cabarete — at the door
  • Puerto Plata airport (POP) — ~20 min
  • 27 Waterfalls of Damajagua (canyoning) — ~45 min
  • Sosúa beaches & snorkeling — ~15 min
  • Playa Grande golf (Río San Juan) — ~1h
  • Jarabacoa — whitewater rafting & hikes — ~2h
  • Samaná (whales, from mid-Jan) — ~3h

Where We Could Stay

Playa Grande vs. Sea Horse Ranch

Playa Grande wins on vibe: design-forward, curated and magazine-worthy, with an on-site restaurant & pool and golf next door. Tradeoffs — bungalows rather than one villa, a wild Atlantic surf beach (dramatic, not always calm for swimming), and a bit more remote (~1½h from Puerto Plata).

Sea Horse Ranch wins on activities & easy group living: one big villa with chef & staff, calm swimming coves, and the closest base to Cabarete kite/surf and the Damajagua falls (~20 min from the airport). Tradeoff — villa-standard rather than designed.

Puerto Rico

Royal Isabela, Vieques & the mountains · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: the easy, no-passport trip that still delivers a real working-farm estate. ~4h nonstop from Boston, then a short drive or hop. The standout is Royal Isabela — freestanding casitas with private plunge pools on 200-ft Atlantic cliffs, a farm-to-table restaurant cooking daily from the estate's own River Farm and organic garden, and a dramatic oceanfront course (a bonus for the golfers).

Two more directions if you want them: Vieques, where Finca Victoria is a design-forward, plant-based guesthouse beside an 18,000-acre wildlife refuge and the world's brightest bioluminescent bay; or the cool central mountains for working coffee haciendas, waterfalls and river swims. (Sam's family has done El Yunque, so this skips the standard rainforest circuit.)

The Area — Cliffs, Islands & Mountains

Three possible bases: Royal Isabela on the north-west cliffs (~1½h from San Juan), Vieques off the east coast (a 22-min hop), and coffee country in the central mountains. SJU is ~4h nonstop from Boston.

  • Royal Isabela (NW cliffs) — ~1½h from San Juan
  • Finca Victoria, Vieques — 22-min hop from SJU
  • Mosquito bioluminescent Bay (Vieques) — brightest in the world
  • Coffee country (Jayuya / Utuado) — central mountains
  • Cerro de Punta & Toro Negro forest — the highlands
  • San Juan airport (SJU) — ~4h nonstop

Grenada — Spice Island

St. George's & the south · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: the "Spice Island" — a design/wellness resort and a boutique beach classic, wrapped around organic cocoa and spice estates, tree-to-bar chocolate, rainforest waterfalls and quiet beaches. The estates here are modern and locally owned (Belmont Estate), so it's a real working-farm experience, farm-to-table, and off the mainstream track. Probably the nicest blend of design, hiking, diving/snorkeling and wellness.

Base at Six Senses La Sagesse or the boutique Calabash Hotel on the calm southern beaches, and fill the days with the cocoa & spice estates, waterfall hikes and reefs. ~6h from Boston (usually one stop; a seasonal nonstop has run from Logan).

The Area — Cocoa, Spice & Sea

A small, green island: beaches and the two resorts in the south near St. George's, the cocoa & spice estates and rainforest in the north and centre. Maurice Bishop airport (GND) is by the beaches.

  • Six Senses La Sagesse (SE beaches) — ~25 min
  • Calabash Hotel (L'Anse aux Épines) — near St. George's
  • Belmont Estate (organic cocoa & spice) — ~1h north
  • Grand Étang rainforest & Seven Sisters Falls — ~40 min
  • Grand Anse beach & sculpture park — ~15 min
  • Maurice Bishop airport (GND) — ~15 min

Where We Could Stay

British Virgin Islands

Virgin Gorda · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Caribbean Puerto Rico Grenada British Virgin Islands Dominican Republic Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende

The vibe: quiet-island living on Virgin Gorda — the BVI's most beautiful island, home to the boulder-and-grotto beaches of The Baths and a pair of understated, nature-first resorts. Design-forward and low-key, with sailing, snorkeling and empty cays a short hop away.

The two bases are Rosewood Little Dix Bay (Laurance Rockefeller's original, reborn) and the villa-and-marina world of Oil Nut Bay. For a whole private island instead, Guana Island off Tortola is an 850-acre nature sanctuary (below). No nonstop — fly to San Juan or St. Thomas, then a short hop or ferry (~7h door-to-door); passports needed.

The Area — Virgin Gorda & the Cays

Virgin Gorda sits near the top of the BVI chain. Fly to San Juan (SJU) or St. Thomas (STT), then a short flight or ferry to Virgin Gorda (VIJ). The Baths, sailing and empty cays are close by.

  • Rosewood Little Dix Bay — Virgin Gorda
  • Oil Nut Bay — Virgin Gorda's east end
  • The Baths (boulders & grottoes) — iconic
  • Gorda Peak National Park hike — island high point
  • Guana Island (private-island alt) — off Tortola
  • Virgin Gorda airport (VIJ) — hop / ferry from SJU/STT
