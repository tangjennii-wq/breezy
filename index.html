Winter Break

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: not a generic resort vacation — we want places that feel curated, design-forward, immersed in nature and experiential, where the destination itself is the experience (Babylonstoren) rather than just a base for excursions. The options fall into two directions: Pacific Mexico (the strongest fits) and the Caribbean & East Coast (shorter flights). This page is the general overview — open any section above to deep-dive each destination.

Pacific Mexico

Longer flights, but the strongest fits for the brief.

  • Careyes / Cuixmala — private architectural estates on a wild coastline, curated and singular. Cuixmala is all nature & wildlife: its own private biosphere reserve, three private beaches, lagoon tours, kayaking, hiking, and a 5,000-acre biodynamic farm with true farm-to-table dining. Casitas, haciendas & villas — inquiry submitted, availability pending (might need two houses).
  • Todos Santos / El Pescadero — Baja surf town meets boutique design hotel; artsy, desert, farm-to-table, slow.
  • San Miguel de Allende — the highlands option: a colonial city of architecture, food, art and courtyards, on a real wine route. More cultural than coastal.
  • Los Cabos (Acre) — the resort end (beach clubs, golf, pools, spas), but Acre is a genuinely good fit for the goal: a communal 4BR farm Hacienda (sleeps 10, available for our week, ~$16.6k / 6 nights) on a working organic farm campus, with golf ~15 min.

Also on the radar but doesn't seem to be the vibe: Riviera Maya / Tulum, the US Virgin Islands (Lovango), and Turks & Caicos.

Caribbean & East Coast

The shorter flights from Boston (~4h nonstop for PR & DR).

  • Puerto Rico (Royal Isabela & Vieques) — a working farm-estate of cliff-top casitas with farm-to-table dining, plus the design guesthouse Finca Victoria on Vieques; ~4h nonstop, no passport.
  • Dominican Republic (Playa Grande & Sea Horse Ranch) — a design-forward beach base (the storybook Playa Grande bungalows) or a roomier full-service villa (Sea Horse Ranch), with waterfalls, reefs and rafting; ~4h nonstop.
  • Grenada (Six Senses La Sagesse & Calabash) — a design/wellness resort with an organic garden, plus locally-owned cocoa & spice estates, rainforest waterfalls and quiet beaches. A plus: Six Senses has a 4BR Cliff Retreat (sleeps 10) — a true communal house on the resort campus, ~$69k for 6 nights.

Also on the radar: St. Lucia — Sugar Beach, a Viceroy resort between the Pitons, with Cabot Saint Lucia's golf course on the island and a lot of activities — more resort vibes. And Dominica — a great spot (Secret Bay), but nothing available for our week. And the British Virgin Islands (Virgin Gorda) — beautiful, with Guana Island as a Babylonstoren-adjacent private island, but zero golf in the BVI; the deep-dive is saved here.

Careyes & Cuixmala

Costalegre, Pacific coast, Jalisco, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: Private architectural estates on a wild coastline — quiet, curated and singular. Horses, nature preserves, empty beaches and dramatic scenery; more a private estate than a hotel.

The wild-and-artful coast: 35,000 acres of protected reserve, nine miles of empty beaches, swimmable coves, and a bohemian community of clifftop "ocean castles" and casitas. It's the purest casita answer — and the most remote, so it rewards settling in and staying put.

Below: an area map with drive times, where to stay (Careyes villas, the Cuixmala estate, and Las Alamandas), the water and wildlife, a proposed week, and an honest budget — this is the priciest finalist.

Priorities check — Home base: Cuixmala is the most Babylonstoren-like option on the entire list (a biodynamic farm estate in its own reserve); the Careyes villas put everyone under one roof with the beach club & polo minutes away. Golf: El Tamarindo (Four Seasons) ~20 min ✓ Getting around: the estate is vast — golf carts, staff-driven safari trucks, and rental cars or bikes; you won't be walking between the beaches and the farm.

The Area — Beaches, Wildlife & More

A remote, spread-out coast. Manzanillo (ZLO, ~1–1½h) is the nearest airport; Puerto Vallarta (PVR, ~2½–3h) is the other gateway, off-map to the north.

  • Beaches & coves (Playa Rosa, Careyitos) — at the door
  • Chamela-Cuixmala reserve safari — on the estate
  • Careyes Polo Club & beach club — ~10 min
  • Copa del Sol (boat or drive) — short hop

  • El Tamarindo golf (Four Seasons) — ~20 min
  • Las Alamandas — ~40 min north
  • Manzanillo airport (ZLO) — ~1–1½h

Los Cabos

Baja California Sur, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: The resort end of the spectrum — beach clubs, golf, pools, spas and restaurants, where the hotel is the destination. The most conventional option (kept for easy group villas).

The easiest trip of the finalists — a ~6½h nonstop from Boston, no red-eye, no connection — with the best food-and-farm scene and, thanks to the Sea of Cortez, the strongest water of any Mexico option. The trade-off is cost: it's the priciest finalist at peak.

The lead here is now Acre: its 4 Bedroom Hacienda (sleeps 10) is available for our dates — one communal house on the working organic farm, with the Michelin-green restaurant on the property. Otherwise, a private villa in the gated communities (Pedregal, Palmilla, Cabo del Sol). The quieter Todos Santos / El Pescadero pocket is its own section.

Priorities check — Home base: Acre's 4BR Hacienda nails it — a communal house on a farm campus you can wander (gardens, animals, the restaurant). The corridor villas are the classic alternative; Casa Brooks sits inside the golf-cart-around Cabo del Sol community. Golf: the easiest fit anywhere — Puerto Los Cabos ~15 min from Acre; several championship courses 15–40 min ✓ Getting around: Acre's farm campus is fully walkable — restaurant, bar, pool and gardens all on foot; a car only to leave the property.

The Area — Beaches, Reef, Farms & Golf

Most stays cluster along the corridor between San José del Cabo and Cabo San Lucas; Todos Santos is ~1h up the Pacific. Los Cabos airport (SJD) is central.

  • Acre farm & restaurant — ~15 min from San José
  • San José del Cabo (art town) — ~15–20 min
  • Chileno & Santa María snorkel bays — ~20–30 min
  • Puerto Los Cabos golf — ~15 min
  • Pedregal / Cabo San Lucas — ~30–40 min

Todos Santos & El Pescadero

Baja California Sur, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: Baja surf town meets boutique design hotel — artsy and laid-back, desert landscapes, farm-to-table, and a slower pace than Cabo.

The quiet, design-forward side of Baja: an hour up the Pacific from the Los Cabos airport, Todos Santos and neighboring El Pescadero trade the Cabo villa-and-golf scene for organic farms, empty surf beaches and understated design hotels under the Sierra de la Laguna. Same easy ~6½h nonstop from Boston — a completely different feel.

For eight you'd take a small design-hotel — Paradero, Villa Santa Cruz or Rancho Pescadero. It's the most on-brief Baja option: farm-driven, low-key, and not a resort bubble.

Priorities check — Home base: the farm hotels (Paradero, Villa Santa Cruz, Rancho Pescadero) are wander-around campuses with pools & daily activities, but separate rooms rather than one shared house — Villa Santa Cruz's multi-BR villas get closest to one roof. Golf: Rancho San Lucas & Solmar Golf Links (both Greg Norman, oceanfront) ~40–45 min down the Todos Santos highway ✓ Getting around: the farm hotels are compact and walkable on-property; a car for Cerritos beach, town and excursions.

The Area — Beach, Farms & Town

A small farm-and-surf pocket on the Pacific, ~1h up from the Los Cabos airport (SJD). El Pescadero and Todos Santos are ~15 min apart.

  • El Pescadero farms & hotels — at the door
  • Cerritos surf beach — ~10 min
  • Todos Santos town (galleries, Hotel California) — ~15 min
  • Sierra de la Laguna hikes — ~30–45 min
  • Los Cabos airport (SJD) — ~1h
  • Rancho San Lucas & Solmar golf — ~40–45 min
  • Cabo San Lucas / corridor — ~1h

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: A colonial-city escape — architecture, food, art and courtyards, boutique hotels. More cultural than nature-focused.

Our first pick, and the easiest to make work: a beautiful, walkable town with wonderful food, a real wine route on the doorstep, comfortable dry-season weather, and simple logistics for a group — all without feeling like a resort.

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: a design-forward beach base on the quiet north coast, with a whole lot of water and adventure at the door — waterfalls, reefs, kite/surf, horseback and a whitewater day-trip. Two very different homes for the week, and the choice comes down to vibe vs. easy group living.

Lead pick — Playa Grande Beach Club: the design choice, and the most on-brief. Nine storybook bungalows by the Kemble family on a wild mile-long Atlantic beach at Río San Juan, with its own restaurant, pool and the Robert Trent Jones golf course next door. Or Sea Horse Ranch: the roomier, full-service alternative — one big oceanfront villa with a private pool, staff and in-villa chef, in a gated community with a beach club, calmer swimming coves and quicker access to the Cabarete/Damajagua adventure hub. Less designed, but easier for a group and better for swimming.

Best of both — a split stay: 3 nights at Playa Grande on the coast, then 2 nights at a boutique mountain lodge in Jarabacoa for whitewater rafting, waterfalls and cool mountain air.

Priorities check — Home base: Sea Horse Ranch is one big staffed villa inside a 250-acre community with beach club, pools, tennis & stables to roam; Playa Grande is a mini-campus of bungalows with its own restaurant & pool. Puerto Rico

Royal Isabela, Vieques & the mountains · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: the easy, no-passport trip that still delivers a real working-farm estate. ~4h nonstop from Boston, then a short drive or hop. The standout is Royal Isabela — freestanding casitas with private plunge pools on 200-ft Atlantic cliffs, a farm-to-table restaurant cooking daily from the estate's own River Farm and organic garden, and a dramatic oceanfront course (a bonus for the golfers).

Two more directions if you want them: Vieques, where Finca Victoria is a design-forward, plant-based guesthouse beside an 18,000-acre wildlife refuge and the world's brightest bioluminescent bay; or the cool central mountains for working coffee haciendas, waterfalls and river swims. Priorities check — Home base: Royal Isabela is a true estate campus to roam (farm, cliffs, pool, spa) — the catch is the casitas sleep two each, so the shared hangout is the restaurant & pool rather than one living room. Golf: the best answer on the list — the oceanfront course is on the estate ✓✓ Getting around: the estate is spread along the cliffs — golf carts between the casitas, restaurant and course; a car to explore Isabela's beaches and caves. Villa Montaña's village is all on foot.

The Area — Cliffs, Islands & Mountains

Three possible bases: Royal Isabela on the north-west cliffs (~1½h from San Juan), Vieques off the east coast (a 22-min hop), and coffee country in the central mountains. SJU is ~4h nonstop from Boston.
