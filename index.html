Careyes & Cuixmala

Costalegre, Pacific coast, Jalisco, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: private architectural estates on a wild coastline — 35,000 acres of protected reserve, nine miles of empty beaches, swimmable coves. Quiet, curated and singular. The most remote of the finalists (~7–8h fly + 1½–3h drive) and the priciest, so it rewards settling in.

Top stay: Cuixmala — the estate itself.

  • One house for everyone? Cuixmala is the most Babylonstoren-like place on the entire list — a biodynamic farm estate in its own reserve; the Careyes villas are the one-roof route.
  • Golf? El Tamarindo (Four Seasons) ~20 min ✓
  • Getting around? The estate is vast — golf carts, staff-driven safari trucks, and rental cars or bikes.

The Area — Beaches, Wildlife & More

A remote, spread-out coast. Manzanillo (ZLO, ~1–1½h) is the nearest airport; Puerto Vallarta (PVR, ~2½–3h) is the other gateway, off-map to the north.

  • Beaches & coves (Playa Rosa, Careyitos) — at the door
  • Chamela-Cuixmala reserve safari — on the estate
  • Careyes Polo Club & beach club — ~10 min
  • Copa del Sol (boat or drive) — short hop
  • Tenacatita snorkel cove — ~30 min
  • El Tamarindo golf (Four Seasons) — ~20 min
  • Manzanillo airport (ZLO) — ~1–1½h

Where We Could Stay

Playa Grande vs. Sea Horse Ranch

Playa Grande wins on vibe: design-forward, curated and magazine-worthy, with an on-site restaurant & pool and golf next door. Tradeoffs — bungalows rather than one villa, a wild Atlantic surf beach (dramatic, not always calm for swimming), and a bit more remote (~1½h from Puerto Plata).

Sea Horse Ranch wins on activities & easy group living: one big villa with chef & staff, calm swimming coves, and the closest base to Cabarete kite/surf and the Damajagua falls (~20 min from the airport). Tradeoff — villa-standard rather than designed.
