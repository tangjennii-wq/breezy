Longer flights, but the strongest fits for the brief.

Caribbean & East Coast

The shorter flights from Boston (~4h nonstop for PR & DR).

  • Puerto Rico (Royal Isabela) — a working farm-estate on Atlantic cliffs with golf on-site; ~4h nonstop, no passport.
  • Dominican Republic (Playa Grande & Sea Horse Ranch) — design bungalows or a staffed villa campus, adventure everywhere; ~4h nonstop.
  • Grenada (Six Senses) — design/wellness resort with cocoa & spice estates; its 4BR Cliff Retreat is a true communal house (sleeps 8 adults).

Best Hikes

The standout trail near each option, ordered by an overall hiking score (★★★★★ = best) — a consistent read across all seven, weighing trail variety, scenery, accessibility from the house and how family-friendly it is. Open the AllTrails link for the official map, distance and difficulty. “≈N trails nearby” is an approximate count of worthwhile mapped trails within about an hour (per AllTrails); the number in brackets is that specific trail's review count — a popularity signal, not a quality score.

  • Puerto Rico — El Yunque Trail  ★★★★★ · ≈10+ trails nearby — a full-day climb through the only tropical rainforest in the U.S. forest system to the misty peak, inside a large, well-maintained network with easier options for everyone; by far the most-hiked trail of any option here. AllTrails · El Yunque Trail [2,228] · all El Yunque trails
  • Dominican Republic — Pico Duarte  ★★★★★ · ≈6 trails nearby — the Caribbean's highest peak (3,098 m), a serious multi-day guided trek from La Ciénaga near Jarabacoa; the best raw hiking of any option, though the summit is an expedition rather than a casual walk. Gentler nearby options: the 27 Charcos de Damajagua river-scramble and the Jimenoa & Baiguate waterfalls. AllTrails · Pico Duarte [86]
  • Grenada — Seven Sisters Waterfalls  ★★★★★ · ≈6 trails nearby — a rainforest walk in Grand Étang National Park to a chain of falls and pools you can jump into, part of a decent network of rainforest and crater-lake trails (Mount Qua Qua, Concord & Annandale falls); short but atmospheric. AllTrails · Seven Sisters [190]
  • Los Cabos — Fox Canyon (Cañón de la Zorra)  ★★★★★ · ≈5 trails nearby — a shaded canyon walk in the Sierra de la Laguna foothills to the Sol de Mayo waterfall and swimming pools; easy, rewarding and very family-friendly, ~1h from Acre. AllTrails · La Zorra Canyon [123]
  • Todos Santos / El Pescadero — Sierra de la Laguna  ★★★★★ · ≈3 trails nearby — palm-canyon backcountry rising into pine-oak highlands above the desert; the San Dionisio canyon route is the classic, though more remote and less maintained than the Cabo side. AllTrails · Sierra de la Laguna [27]
  • San Miguel de Allende — Peña de Bernal  ★★★★★ · ≈5 trails nearby — a short steep scramble up one of the world's tallest monoliths, an easy day trip on the wine route, plus the in-town Charco del Ingenio canyon-and-botanical-garden loop; pleasant day walks rather than immersive wilderness. AllTrails · Peña de Bernal [256] · Charco del Ingenio [67]
  • Careyes / Cuixmala  ★★★★★ · ≈2 trails nearby — mostly a private coastal nature reserve, so mapped trails are limited; the Cerro Careyes viewpoint is the main marked walk, and most nature outings here are guided within the estate. AllTrails · Cerro Careyes [6]

On the radar: the BVI's best hike is Gorda Peak on Virgin Gorda — a quick forest climb to a 360° lookout, and about the only real trail on the island.

Careyes & Cuixmala

Costalegre, Pacific coast, Jalisco, Mexico · Dec 26, 2026 – Jan 2, 2027 · eight of us

Overview Pacific Careyes / Cuixmala Los Cabos Todos Santos San Miguel de Allende Caribbean Puerto Rico Dominican Republic Grenada

The vibe: private architectural estates on a wild coastline — 35,000 acres of protected reserve, nine miles of empty beaches, swimmable coves. Quiet, curated and singular. The most remote of the finalists (~7–8h fly + 1½–3h drive) and the priciest, so it rewards settling in.

Top stay: Cuixmala

Further reading: Condé Nast Traveler · Travel + Leisure · Costalegre, Mexico's "Happy Coast"

  • One house for everyone? Cuixmala is the most Babylonstoren-like place on the entire list — a biodynamic farm estate in its own reserve; the Careyes villas are the one-roof route.
  • Golf? El Tamarindo (Four Seasons) ~40 min ✓ (per guests, longer than maps suggest)
  • Getting around? The estate is vast — golf carts, staff-driven safari trucks, and rental cars or bikes.
  • Biggest negatives? Hardest trip of the finalists (12–15h door-to-door), priciest, non-refundable holiday terms — and the casitas sit inland from the famous house.

The Honest Take

From guest reviews — TripAdvisor, Reddit & forums. What people love, and what they complain about.

  • Loved: “Service bordering on telepathic”; repeat guests say it beats One&Only/St. Regis at similar rates — “Cuixmala has ruined all other vacations for me.” The true anti-resort.
  • The casitas aren't the famous house: they sit ~15–20 min inland from beachfront Casa Cuixmala, with their own (smaller) pool scene — confirm exactly what we'd get before wiring money.
  • Value gripes: $80 beach-club lunches, repetitive menus across two estate restaurants over a week (no town alternative), and a ~$130/day on-property car you need to reach the swimmable cove.
  • Booking terms: holiday weeks are 100% prepaid and non-refundable; reviewers report a cancellation-hostile operator.
  • Rustic realities: partial A/C, sand fleas at the beach club, wildlife noise — and only ~89 TripAdvisor reviews at this price point.
  • The travel day: realistically 12–15h door-to-door each way with connections + a 1½–3h drive; golf is ~40 min, one course only.

Top stay: Acre — the 4-bedroom farm Hacienda.

Further reading: Condé Nast Traveler · Travel + Leisure

  • One house for everyone? Yes — Acre's 4-bedroom farm Hacienda (sleeps 10) is available for our dates, right on the farm campus.

The Honest Take

From guest reviews — TripAdvisor, Reddit & forums. What people love, and what they complain about.

  • Loved: “One of the most unique properties in the world”; the restaurant runs Google 4.6 across 1,800+ reviews; a Dec guest called it “genuinely magical.”
  • Noise is the #1 complaint: weddings/events (11pm curfew), then village dogs and roosters. New Year's week is peak party season — ask exactly what events are booked Dec 26–Jan 2 before paying. The Hacienda's real walls and A/C help a lot vs. the open treehouses.
  • Not on the beach: the farm is inland; Veleros beach club is ~10 min (~$40/pp with food credit) — a daily shuttle for eight.

  • Holiday congestion: Cabo runs at max capacity that week — tee times and Flora Farms tables book out weeks ahead.
  • Kinks at the price: reviewers note on-demand hot water, slow Wi-Fi and occasional service confusion.
